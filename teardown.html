01  /  Overview

Executive Summary

01

This teardown analyses the onboarding process of Relay.app, an AI-powered workflow automation platform founded in 2021 by Jacob Bank (ex-Director of PM, Gmail & Calendar; co-founded Timeful, acquired by Google). Relay has achieved a 4.9★ G2 rating, secured $8.1M in seed funding from Khosla Ventures and a16z, and connects 100+ apps.

The platform’s core differentiator is its human-in-the-loop approach — combining AI-speed automation with manual approval steps at critical decision points, a gap left open by Zapier, Make.com, and n8n. Recent AI-personalized onboarding (LinkedIn scan + experience-level question) can compress time-to-value to approximately 60 seconds for many users.

This analysis reveals three high-leverage improvement areas: app connection friction (Step 4 drop-off), approval step under-education (the core differentiator is missed by many first-time users), and the absence of an onboarding progress checklist that would lift activation rates by 10–15%.

05  /  Psychology

PSYCH Framework Analysis

05

The PSYCH framework (by Darius Contractor, VP Growth @ Airtable) models every user as starting with a fixed amount of motivation to complete the onboarding journey. Every step either adds to or subtracts from that score. When total psych drops below ~50, conversion probability drops dramatically. The goal: keep psych above the danger threshold at every stage.

✓  Strong +Psych Signals
  • Clear ‘Start for free’ CTA — no credit card required
  • Social proof: G2 4.9★ + recognisable logos (Ramp, Motion…)
  • 1-click Google sign-in removes account-creation barrier
  • Clean, uncluttered UI reduces cognitive load at every step
  • Personalised templates create instant ‘made for me’ feeling
  • Successful first run → large +Psych spike (Aha Moment)
⚠  Major −Psych Friction Points
  • App connection: which app? what permissions? why now? — no guidance
  • Approval step value unclear for first-time users — missed differentiator
  • Anxiety before first workflow run (“will this actually work?”)
  • No progress checklist — users don’t know how close they are to success
  • Limited role-specific templates force building from scratch

06  /  Analysis

Key Learnings from the Onboarding

06
UX & Product Design Choices
AI-First Onboarding
Instead of generic tours, Relay scans LinkedIn + asks experience level → shows tailored agent templates immediately. Personalisation can reduce time-to-first-value to ~60 seconds for many users — a paradigm shift where the product does the work instead of the user.
Time-to-Value as a Design Goal
The entire flow is optimised to deliver a working agent/workflow in under 60 seconds for many users. This directly lowers abandonment before the Aha Moment and sets a new benchmark for PLG onboarding in the automation space.
Human-in-the-Loop as Core Philosophy
Approvals are a first-class step type in the builder, reinforcing that humans stay in control — a genuine differentiator vs. Zapier & Make. However, this differentiator is currently undersold during onboarding, leaving adoption lower than it should be.
Growth & Psychology
✓  What Works Well
  • Clear social proof (G2, trusted logos) from first touchpoint
  • Frictionless Google sign-in keeps entry motivation high
  • LinkedIn-powered personalisation creates an emotional “made for me” moment
  • Visual builder reduces cognitive load vs. code-first tools
  • Transparent pricing avoids “credit wall” surprises that damage trust
⚠  What Needs Fixing
  • No checklist — users don’t know how far they’ve come or how close they are
  • App connection decision paralysis erodes momentum at the worst moment
  • Approval step’s value is invisible to first-time users — core differentiator wasted
  • Pre-run anxiety at Step 7 brings psych dangerously close to the abandonment threshold
07  /  Improvements

Recommendations to Improve Onboarding

07
1
Add an Onboarding Checklist
Show a persistent progress tracker: Create account → Connect app → Build workflow → Add approval → Run first workflow. Each completed step adds positive reinforcement and creates momentum toward completion.
Why it works: Gives users a clear path and adds small wins at each step — directly boosting +Psych and reducing drop-off. Industry data shows checklists improve onboarding completion by 10–20%.
2
Guide App Connection More Tightly
Use LinkedIn + experience data to recommend a single “best first app” (e.g. Gmail for PMs, HubSpot for sales). Add permission explanations (“Relay reads X to do Y”) and show which workflow templates unlock after connecting.
Why it works: +15–20% reduction in drop-off at Step 4. Raises PSYCH score at this juncture from 70 → 85, preventing the first and largest funnel leak.
3
Educate Better on Approval Steps
Trigger a short in-context tutorial on first approval step addition with 2–3 concrete role-specific use cases. Consider making it a required step in the first guided workflow so every new user experiences the core differentiator at least once.
Why it works: +25–35% increase in approval step adoption. Users who use approval steps show higher retention — they’re experiencing the product’s unique moat, not a generic automation tool.
4
Enrich Templates & Collaboration
Ship role/industry-specific template packs with approvals already built-in: Product, CS, Sales, Marketing, HR. Add a direct “Invite teammate” CTA with simple sharing permissions in the collaborator panel to drive team-wide adoption.
Why it works: +10–15% activation rate improvement. Reduces time to first workflow from 10 min to <5 min. Team invitations within 7 days are a strong signal of long-term retention.
