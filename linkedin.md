**1/4 | I tore down Relay.app's onboarding — here's what a PM should never let ship**

Relay.app promises to automate your workflows with AI while keeping humans in the loop.

So I signed up as a first-time user and mapped every step — from landing page to first successful run.

8 steps. 3 friction points. 1 massive missed opportunity.

Here's what I found 👇

---

**What Relay.app absolutely nails:**

→ AI-personalized onboarding — it scans your LinkedIn profile and asks your experience level to surface role-specific templates in seconds

→ Time-to-value in ~60 seconds — one of the fastest activation flows I've seen in B2B SaaS

→ Google sign-in + minimal form = near-zero signup friction

---

**Where users quietly drop off:**

→ **Step 4 — App Connection:** No guidance on which app to connect first, no preview of what unlocks after. Users stall here with zero context.

→ **Step 6 — Approval Step:** Relay's biggest differentiator (human-in-the-loop) is buried in the builder with no explanation for first-timers. Most users skip it entirely.

→ **No onboarding checklist:** Users have no sense of progress or what "done" looks like.

---

**My top 3 recommendations:**

1. Add a persistent progress tracker → Create → Connect → Build → Run. Small wins build momentum.

2. Use the LinkedIn + experience data you already collected to recommend ONE app to connect first — don't make users guess.

3. Make the approval step mandatory in the first guided workflow. Every new user should experience the differentiator at least once.

---

**The North Star Metric I'd watch:**

**Workflow Activation Rate** — % of new signups who create and successfully run at least one workflow in their first session.

If that number is low, nothing else matters.
